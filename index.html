✈️ Flight Emissions

Formula:
Flight emissions (kg CO₂e) = distance × 1.1 × emission factor
Where:
  • distance = great-circle distance between airports (shortest path on Earth)
  • 1.1 = detour factor (adds 10% for indirect routing, holding patterns, ATC paths)
  • Emission factors by flight type:
    • Domestic (<500 km): 0.255 kg/km
    • Short-haul (500–3,700 km): 0.156 kg/km
    • Long-haul (>3,700 km): 0.150 kg/km
  • Factors represent average climate impact per passenger-km (includes fuel burn, occupancy, NOx, contrails at altitude)
Aligned with: DEFRA GHG Conversion Factors, ICAO aviation methodology, IPCC aviation research.

🏨 Accommodation Emissions

Formula:
Hotel emissions (kg CO₂e) = nights × 30.9
Where:
  • 30.9 kg CO₂e per night = estimated average derived from Cornell Hotel Sustainability Benchmark data and related hospitality emission studies.
Informed by: Cornell Hotel Sustainability Benchmark, global hospitality emissions studies.

🚌 Bus / Shared Transport Emissions

Formula:
Bus emissions (kg CO₂e) = distance × 0.089 kg/km
Where:
  • 0.089 kg CO₂e per passenger-km = average emissions for shared vehicle transport (vans, minibuses, tour buses).
Consistent with: IPCC and DEFRA international transport emission datasets.

🚗 Passenger Car Emissions

Car emissions are calculated by multiplying the total distance traveled (km) by an emission factor of 0.171 kg CO₂e per km. This factor represents the average emissions of a standard passenger vehicle including fuel combustion and associated greenhouse gases.

Formula:
CO₂e (kg) = Distance (km) × 0.171
Example:
A 500 km car journey = 500 × 0.171 = 85.5 kg CO₂e
Source: DEFRA/EEA.
